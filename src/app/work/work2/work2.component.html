Impact

Victims of online misogyny experience a sense of fear and attack on their integrity. Amnesty international found that of the woman who experienced abuse or harassment online, 41% of responding woman felt that their physical safety was threatened and 1 in 2 woman experienced lower self-esteem or loss of self confidence as well as stress, anxiety or panic attacks.

The chartered Institutes of Marketers, Uk reported that 83% of woman had self-censored. This means that honest and open democratic conversation is being threatened. Woman fought hard for the right to vote - we cannot let ourselves be chased out of political engagement!
