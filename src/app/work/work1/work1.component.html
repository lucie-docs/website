Why we exists

We exists because woman are disproportionally suffering threatening and abusive behavior online - simple because they dare to voice their opinions. This sexist abuse violates human rights: including the right to security of person, the right to non-discrimination, and the right to freedom of expresion and opinion.

Online Misogyny

Online sexual harassment, hate-speech, online VAWG (violence against woman and girls), technologically mediated violence... there are many names for what we are fighting against!

There are also multiple definitions. What is offensive or abusive to one woman might not be for the next. Hate speech is nuanced, and what can be abuse in one context, may be non-threatening in another.

Victims of online misogyny experience a sense of fear and attack on their integrity. Amnesty international found that of woman who experience abuse or harassment online, 41% od responding woman felt that their physical safety was threatened and 1 in 2 woman experienced low self-esteem or loss of self confidence as well as stress, anxiety or panic attacks.

The Chartered Institute of Marketeers UK reported that 83% of woman had self-censored when posting opinions online in ordered to avoid harassment. This means that honest and open democratic conversation is being threatened. Woman fought hard for the right to vote - we cannot let ourselves be chased out of political engagement.
